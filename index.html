About

"Movement is my mother tongue — and the whole world is my stage."

Born in Belo Horizonte, Brazil, and forged by one of South America's most rigorous professional dance institutions, Carla Sandra Silva has built a two-decade career that spans classical ballet, contemporary choreography, and prime-time television production across Europe and the Americas.

Since making Germany her home, Carla has performed on the stages of the country's most watched networks — ZDF, RTL, ARD, VOX — and alongside international artists including Oliver Tree, Tokio Hotel, and Mark Foster. She choreographed and directed the UEFA WM Opening Show at the iconic Brandenburger Tor in Berlin, and captivated audiences at Arena Dortmund in 2026.

As a creative director, she brings architectural vision to every project — translating emotion, music, and culture into movement that resonates across borders. As a teacher, she has shaped hundreds of students in Germany, Austria, Switzerland, and Brazil.
